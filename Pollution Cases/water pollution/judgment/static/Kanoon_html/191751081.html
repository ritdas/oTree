                                   ORAL JUDGMENT

(PER : HONOURABLE THE ACTING CHIEF JUSTICE MR. VIJAY MANOHAR SAHAI)

1. We have heard Ms.G.R.Vijayalakshmi, learned advocate for the petitioner, Mr.Parth Bhatt, learned AGP for respondent Nos. 1 to 2 and Mr.Biren A.Vaishnav, learned advocate for respondent Nos. 3 to 4.

2. By way of this Writ Petition in the nature of public interest litigation, the petitioner has prayed for the following reliefs:

"(A) That the Hon'ble Court may be pleased to issue appropriate writ, order or direction in the nature of certiorari or mandamus or any other appropriate writ, order or direction directing the respondents to stop storage of coal in the coal storage yard.
(B) Your lordships may be pleased to direct the respondents to cover the coal storage yard with tarpaulin with correct size tarpaulin which could cover the cold storage yard with adequate safety measures;
(C) Your lordship may be pleased to direct the respondents to have water test to assess the water pollution causing due to the present plant."

3. In the affidavit-in-reply filed on behalf of respondent Nos. 3 and 4, it has been stated that necessary action has been taken by the Gujarat Pollution Control Board instructing respondent No.5 Company to comply with the Coal Handling Guidleines and repeated inspections have been carried out periodically by the Officers of the Pollution Control Board. It has further been stated that the officers of the Board are conscious and acting with responsibility and have ensured that sprinklers have been installed to prevent coal dust.

4. Further a similar petition filed by the another resident of the same area on the same grounds has been finally disposed of by the Division Bench of this Court on 22.08.2014 being Special Civil Application No.6882 of 2013, which is extracted below:

"After arguing the case for some time and in view of the nature of subject petition and even disputed question of facts are involved, learned advocate for the petitioners seeks permission to withdraw this petition with a view to take proper remedy including filing of Civil Suit in accordance with law. Disposed of as withdrawn. Notice discharged.
However, withdrawal of petition shall not come in the way of Gujarat Pollution Control Board of taking action in accordance with law in case, if any breach of provision of Air and Water Pollution Act is noticed."

5. In view of the oral statement made by respondent Nos. 3 and 4, this writ petition (PIL) does not survive and deserves to be dismissed. Hence, it is, accordingly, dismissed as sufficient compliance has been carried out by the respondents.
