Madhya Pradesh High Court
Ashish Kumar Shukla vs South Eastern Coal Fields Limited ... on 11 December, 2013
                                      W.P No.17677/13
11.12.2013          Shri Sumit Raghuvanshi, Advocate for the petitioner.
                    Shri Anoop Nair, Advocate for respondent no.1.

Heard counsel for the parties.

The relief claimed in this petition is to issue writ against the respondent-Company to stop diversion work of Baghaiya Nala. According to the petitioner, the attempt of the respondent-Company is to divert the river stream at Baghaiya.

This factual assertion has been refuted by the respondent- Company on affidavit. In addition, the respondent has placed on record the permissions granted by statutory authorities under the provisions of Air and Water Pollution Acts respectively. Even the State Administration (Collector) has permitted the respondent to start the mining activities at the stated places. The argument of the petitioner is that no permission for diversion of the river stream is forthcoming. According to the respondent, there is no attempt of diversion of river stream at all. The Nala is formed on account of seasonal rains as is noticed from the scheme submitted by the respondent to the authorities.

Notably, petitioner has not impleaded the concerned State Authorities including the Pollution Control Board, who would be in a position to respond to the stand taken by the petitioner.

As a result, we are not inclined to entertain this petition. The petitioner is free to take recourse to the proceedings before National Green Tribunal, if so advised, in which all aspects can be considered after giving opportunity to the State Authorities and Pollution Control Board.

Petition is disposed of.

Interim relief is vacated.
