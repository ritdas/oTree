ORAL
JUDGMENT

The present Criminal Revision Application, under section 401 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, is directed against the judgment and order dated 16.01.1998 passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Palitana, in Criminal Case No. 1315 of 1992, whereby the learned Magistrate was pleased to discharge all the respondents accused from the charges levelled against them.

The brief facts of the prosecution case is that the respondent No. 1 is the company, opponent No.2 & 3 respectively are the Chairman and Managing Director of the said company and respondents No.4 to 9 are the Directors and respondent No.10 is the Production Manager of the said company. It is alleged that the respondent No.1 company is manufacturing industrial alcohol. It is also alleged that the appellant Board by its consent order dated 21.2.1986 and 22.7.1991 had laid down conditions for the discharge of effluent and the respondents were required to follow the conditions. It is alleged that on 26.02.1992 the Officers of the Board visited the respondent No.1 company and in presence of respodnent No.10 Production Manager, as per the provisions of the Act, collected the two samples of water being discharged, viz. One from the effluent coming out of the oxidation ditch and the other from the effluent coming out of the dilution tank, and sent the same for analysis. On analysis the Analyst found that the said samples were not conforming to the standards laid down by the Board. Therefore, after obtaining sanction complaint has been filed against the respondents accused. Thereafter, the trial issued summons to the respondents accused. After the evidence of the prosecution was recorded under Section 244 Cr.P.C. The trial Court heard both the sides for framing of the charge against the accused and after hearing the parties the learned Magistrate has discharged all the accused from the offences alleged against them vide order dated 16.01.1998.

3. It was contended by learned Advocate Mr. Sunil Mehta for the appellant that the judgment and order of the learned Magistrate is not proper, legal and it is erroneous. He has also contended that the opponents were discharging the polluted water of their company into the open field in contrvention of the directives of the Board and that this effluent was affecting the soil adversely and was injurious to the cattle. He also contended that at the stage of framing of the charge the learned Magistrate was required to consider whether there was any prima facie case and whether the evidence adduced so far was sufficient to frame charge against the accused. He has contended that the Board Officers have followed the rules prescribed by law and the Officer of the Board have also followed the procedure of taking the sample. The sample was seized and sealed properly. Yet, the learned Magistrate has not considered the evidence of prosecution. Therefore, the order impugned in this appeal passed by the learned Magistrate requires to be quashed and set aside.

Learned Advocate, appearing for the respondents original accused, has supported the judgment and order of acquittal passed by the trial Court and contended that the trial Court has rightly acquitted the respondents accused.

I have gone through the order passed by the trial court. I have also perused the oral as well as documentary evidence led by the trial court and also considered the submissions made by learned Advocate for the appellant. The trial court while considering the oral as well as documentary evidence has clearly observed that there is contradictory version in the evidence of witnesses. The trial Court has also found that the Board Officer has not followed the proper procedure while sending the sample to the Board Analyst. The trial Court has also observed that samples were taken on 26.2.1992 while the same were received by the Board Analyst on 3.3.1992 and, therefore, there is breach of provisions of the Rules. The trial Court has also found that the Board has committed the breach of Section 21(3)(d) and 27 of the Water Pollution Act and, therefore, prima facie the case is not proved against the accused respondents. The trial Court has also considered the various decisions of this Court as well as of the Hon'ble Apex Court and discharged the accused from the offences alleged them. Even in the present appeal, nothing is produced or pointed out to rebut the conclusion of the trial Court. Thus, from the evidence itself it is established that the prosecution has not proved its case beyond reasonable doubt. Looking to the above observation and findings of the trial Court, I am of the opinion that the trial Court has not committed any error or illegality while passing the order of discharge.

At the out-set it is required to be noted that the principles which would govern and regulate the hearing of appeal by this Court against an order passed by the trial Court have been very succinctly explained by the Hon'ble Apex Court in catena of decisions. Thus, the powers which this Court may exercise against an order of acquittal are well settled. Thus, in case the appellate Court agrees with the reasons and the findings given by the lower Court, then the discussion of evidence is not necessary.

I have gone through the Judgment and order passed by the trial Court and also perused the oral as well as documentary evidence produced before me and also considered the submissions made by the learned Advocate for the applicant. On going through the Judgment and order passed by the trial Court and the papers placed before me, I am of the opinion that the trial Court has not committed any error while discharging the respondents accused from the alleged offences. I find that the findings recorded by the trial Court are absolutely just and proper and in recording the said findings no illegality or infirmity has been committed by it. The learned Judge has rightly come to the conclusion that the prosecution has miserably failed to prove the case against the respondent accused.

I am, therefore, in complete agreement with the findings, ultimate conclusion and the resultant order of discharge recorded by the Court below and hence find no reason to interfere with the same. Hence, the Revision Application is hereby dismissed.

In view of above the Revision Application is dismissed. The Judgment and order dated 16.1.1998 passed by the learned Magistrate in Criminal Case No.1315 of 1992, discharging the respondents accused from the offences alleged against them, is hereby confirmed. Rule is discharged. Bail bond, if any, shall stands cancelled.
