                         ORDER

The petitioner is a medical scale industry located at Sy.No.83A and 79 of Belgal village, Bellary taluk and district in an area measuring 11 acres, which forms part of 3599 hectares of land declared by the Government of Karnataka as Industrial land. The petitioner established his industry since 01/12/2010 and he has obtained necessary permission from the concerned department. The petitioner made an application for certificate from the Pollution Control Board under Section 25 of the Water Act and he has got deemed permission under sub section 7 of Section 25 of the Water Act 1974. Despite the same, second respondent-Pollution Control Board passed an order which was challenged before the Appellate Authority in 3 appeal No.16-17/2012. The same was disposed of 31/01/2013 and remanded to the second respondent for reconsideration. The said order was subject matter in Writ petition No.8106/2013 and 8272-74/2013 which was disposed of on 23/07/2013 wherein permission sought by the petitioner to make an application for clearance from the Pollution Control Board was accepted and respondent was directed to dispose of the same.

2. In view of the directions issued by this Court in the above referred writ petition, though an application was made under Section 25 of the Act and necessary documents were produced, the respondent has not disposed of the same till today. In the meanwhile first respondent Gulburga Electricity Supply Company by its instruction dated 10.09.2013 has disconnected power supply given to the petitioner on 08.09.2013 at 5.00 p.m. and the same was communicated to the petitioner on 10th September 2013. Being aggrieved by the same, petitioner has filed this petition.

4

3. The action of the first respondent in disconnecting power supply to the petitioner industry and the action of the second respondent in directing the first respondent to disconnect power supply is subject matter in this writ petition.

4. The ground urged by the petitioner is that since 2010 he has started carrying on business in the factory and he had made an application within the stipulated time i.e. before commencement of the factory and since it was not disposed of within the time stipulated, he has got deemed permission under sub section (7) of Section 25 of the Water Act. He has challenged the direction issued by the second respondent to the first respondent dated 10/09/2013 as ultra vires. It is under Rule 34 sub clause 4 member Secretary to the Board has power to issue such direction contrary to the same Annexure "J" is issued by the Assistant Executive who is not a member Secretary or any person appointed by the 5 Board in that behalf. Secondly, direction issued by the second respondent to the first respondent also not applicable to the occupier of the nature i.e. the petitioner herein. Learned counsel refers to the judgment reported in 2006 SC 2038. The said judgment is referred in order to substantiate his submission that clearance from the Pollution Control Board or various department is dispensed since the land was allotted for industrial purpose by the KSIIDC -Annexure B. Notification issued by the Government of Karnataka dated 01/12/2010 for acquiring land for industrial establishment in which the petitioner commissioned his industry.

5. Learned counsel for the first respondent GESCOM submitted that it is not required to issue notice to the occupier since such opportunities were given by the second respondent. Any direction issued by the concerned authority under the Water Act 1974, first respondent has to comply with the same as per Section 33 A of the Water Act, which is mandatory one, and the first respondent is 6 bound by such direction. The counsel for the second respondent submitted to dismiss this petition as the mandatory requirement under Section 25 of the Water Act has not been complied. Prior permission has to be obtained before establishing any industry. Applications made by the petitioner for clearance have been dismissed on earlier occasions. One such refusal has been produced and marked as Annexure "R2" dated 31/12/2008, "R7" dated 09/10/2012 and "R8" dated 13/09/2013. This refusal order initially has not been challenged. The direction issued by this Court in Writ Petition No.8106/2013 and other connected matters vide Annexure "G" has not been complied since required materials are not produced by the petitioner. Second respondent had addressed several complaints by the public and that the pollution emitted by the petitioner-industry is causing health hazards and agricultural lands have become barren. Hence, it has directed first respondent to disconnect the power supply. Second respondent has also passed an order dated 02/07/2012 for closure as per Annexure "R-6". 7 It is further submitted that it is not only the petitioner industry has been closed down but other 5 industries have also been closed down for want of clearance from the Pollution Control Board.

6. Learned Government Pleader adopted the submission made by respondents 1 and 2.

7. It is undisputed fact that the land on which the petitioner is carrying out business is allotted by the Government for industrial purposes. The purpose of allotment is for establishing industries. Any industry before establishing or commencing its business, has to take prior permission under Section 25 of the Water Act 1974 which reads thus:

"25. Restrictions on new outlets and new discharges: subject to the provisions of this section, no person shall, without the previous consent on the State Board-
(a) establish or take any steps to establish any industry, operation or process, or any treatment and 8 disposal system or any extension or addition thereto, which is likely to discharge sewage or trade effluent into a stream or well or sewer or on land (such discharge being hereafter in this section referred to as discharge of sewage) and an application for consent of State Board under sub-section(1) shall be made in such form, contain such particulars and shall be accompanied by such fees as may be prescribed.

Sub Section (7) of Section 25 states that the consent referred to in sub-section(1) shall, unless, given or refused earlier, be deemed to have been given unconditionally on the expiry of a period of four months of the making of an application in this behalf complete in all respects to the State Board."

8. In the instant case, application made by the petitioner for permission under Section 25 of the Act was not disposed of within the time stipulated. Hence, the petitioner is said to have the benefit of deemed provision. 9

9. The said submission has been examined in the light of the communications made as referred in Annexures R2, R7 and R9. By these endorsement the case of the petitioner for grant of permission was refused. Notwithstanding the same, when closure order was passed, the same was challenged before the Karnataka State Appellate Authority, Bangalore, constituted under the Water Act 1981. Writ Appeal NO.18 and connected appeal no.19/2011 was disposed of on 9th November 2011 in which, order passed by the second respondent was set aside and remanded for fresh consideration. Second respondent has not placed any reliance as to the order passed pursuant to the remand order dated 09/11/2011. Hence, it is taken for recording purpose that the second respondent has not disposed of the matter after the appeals are remanded on 09/11/2011.

10. The case of the first respondent is that he is duty bound to comply with the directions issued by the second respondent is being examined.

10

11. Section 33 A of the Act reads as follows:

33A. Power to give directions:-

Notwithstanding anything contained in any other law, but subject to the provisions of this Act, and to any directions that the Central Government may give in this behalf, a Board may, in the exercise of its powers and performance of this functions under this Act, issue any directions in writing to any person, officer or authority and such person, officer or authority shall be bound to comply with such directions.

As per Section 33A it is the Board who is the competent authority to issue directions to any persons or authority and such directions issued by the competent authority is binding on all other authorities. Rule 34 of the Water Rules 1975 further mandates that the proposed directions issued by the board either for stoppage or regulation of electricity or water or any other services affecting the carrying of any industry, operation or process and is sought to be issued to an officer or an authority, a copy of 11 the proposed direction shall also be endorsed to the occupier of the industry, operation or process, as the case may be, and objections, if any, filed by the occupier with an officer designated in this behalf shall be dealt with in accordance with the procedures under sub-rules(3) and (5) of this rule. From this it is clear that direction can be issued either by the Board or by the Member Secretary of the Board or any person authorised by the Board.

12. Now it is relevant to examine whether the impugned direction issued by the first respondent is by the Environment Officer of the Board at Bellary.

13. In the communication dated 06/09/2013, it is not referred whether the same has been authorised by the Board or whether he is the competent authority or whether he is a Member Secretary. Unless the persons who issue directions to the first respondent is competent, such direction is ultra vires under Section 33A and sub-rule(4) of Rule 34 of the Water Act and Rules. The direction issued to the first respondent is not by the competent 12 authority. In this regard, submission of the second respondent that what has been forwarded to the first respondent is only the communication of the decision taken by the Board and the Member Secretary who are competent. In this regard it is relevant to mention that when a statute directs a statutory authority to function in a particular manner he should carry out the same in the manner prescribed under the Act. The Act says it is the Member Secretary or an authorised person by the Board shall issue direction to the first respondent then he alone can act in accordance with the said provisions. He cannot delegate such power to environment authority since he has no power of delegation. Further the environment officer is not a competent authority to issue direction to the first respondent. The learned counsel for the appellant relies on sub para 2 of para 97 of the judgment in the case of Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board Vs. C. Kenchappa and Ors. reported AIR 2006 SC 2308 97 para(2) which is as follows:

13

"(2) We also direct the appellant to incorporate the condition of allotment to obtain clearance from the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board before the land is allotted for development. The said directory condition of allotment of lands be converted into a mandatory condition for all the projects to be sanctioned in future."

This Court in (1987) 7 Kar. Law Journal 410 held that " as a matter of fact no industry shall be commenced unless the unit secures clearance from the agency under the relevant provisions of the law as aforementioned". The legal provisions thus take care of the possible situation in which the industry, unit established in any one of those industrial area may be found to be violating the law, the units can even be permitted to be closed if they do not comply with the statutory requirement relating to control and elimination of pollution.

14. Industries are very much important for the growth of a nation. Industries provide employment and 14 improve the standard of living and as well as social and economic condition. They are the backbone of the nation and at the same time, health of a man is of paramount importance. Industries shall not be permitted to carry on at the cost of human existence. Closure of industries creates epidemic in the society and creates unemployment. Closure of an industry is not a solution, in effect, it is the Pollution Control Board to get the industry established and take measures to reduce pollution which shall not mar the human growth. In view of the above, case on hand is required to be decided.

15. The application filed by the petitioner as per the direction of this court is still pending consideration. If the materials furnished by the petitioner are not sufficient, the Board has to call for required documents from the petitioner. Unless such directions are issued to the petitioner, it is not proper for the Board to dismiss the case. In the circumstance, the action of the first respondent in disconnecting power on the basis of the 15 direction/communication issued by the incompetent authority is illegal and ultra vires. Hence, I pass the following order:

Writ Petition is allowed. Annexure "J" is hereby quashed. The first respondent forthwith connect the power supply to the petitioner industry. Second respondent is directed to dispose of the applications pending before it afresh within 3 months in accordance with law. If the documents furnished by the petitioner are not sufficient, second respondent shall call for further documents and pass appropriate order in accordance with law within three months from the date of receipt of a certified copy of this order.
