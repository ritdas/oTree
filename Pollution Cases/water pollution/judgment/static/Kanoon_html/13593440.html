                           J U D G M E N T

The writ petition was filed challenging Exts.P14 and P15. Ext.P14 is a direction issued by the Kerala State Pollution Control Board exercising its powers under Section 33A of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 directing that the hotel of the petitioner shall be closed till adequate pollution facilities are provided with the approval of the Board. Ext.P15 is a consequential communication issued by the Panchayat.

2. Apart from contending that the Hotel has its own waste disposal facilities, petitioner has no case that it has installed an effluent treatment plant for discharge of effluents. If that be so, Ext.P14 order issued by the Board cannot be said to be illegal warranting interference, muchless in a proceedings under Article 226 of the Constitution of India.

3. Realising this difficulty, learned counsel for the petitioner today undertakes before this Court that the requirements laid down by the Pollution Control Board will be complied with by them by installing necessary facilities within WPC.No.35020/07 :2 : four months from today.

4. Taking note of this submission and also considering the fact that the petitioner is a running establishment and that this order has been remaining stayed on account of the interim orders passed by this court in this writ petition, I feel the time as sought for should be granted to the petitioner.

Therefore, recording the undertaking of the learned counsel for the petitioner, this writ petition is disposed of directing that the hotel will be permitted to function for four months from today subject to the petitioner obtaining necessary licence from the 1st respondent Panchayat and also getting the facilities installed by them approved by the Board in the meanwhile.
