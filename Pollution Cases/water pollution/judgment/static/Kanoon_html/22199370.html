ORAL ORDER

(PER : HONOURABLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE MR. BHASKAR BHATTACHARYA) By this application under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, the petitioner has prayed for issue of a writ of mandamus or a writ in the nature of mandamus or any other appropriate writ or order quashing and setting aside the notice dated 13th March, 2012, being Annexure "A" to this application, with further direction upon the respondent No.1 to process the application of the petitioner for carrying on some other business in the premises.

It appears that the Gujarat Pollution Control Board, by the impugned notice dated 13th March, 2012, issued under Section 33A of the Water (Prevention of Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, directed closure of the unit of the petitioner, which was carrying on an industry of galvanization.

The grievance of the writ-petitioner in this application is that he has intended to close the aforesaid business, as he intends to start a new unit for engineering or godown purpose with the assurance that there would be no generation of hazardous waste and emission of any gas. The petitioner complaints that in spite of the filing of such an application and undertaking, the Gujarat Pollution Control Board is not processing the same.

Mr.

Vyas, the learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the Gujarat Pollution Control Board submits that if the petitioner wants to start a new business after complying with all the formalities required under the law, his client has no objection in granting permission, provided the previous activities done by the petitioner is not continued.

Such being the position, we direct the Gujarat Pollution Control Board to process the application filed by the petitioner for starting new business as indicated in the undertaking, and to pass necessary order of revocation of the closure notice, if it is satisfied that the petitioner would not carry on his previous business for which closure notice was issued, and the business intended to be done would be started after compliance of the formalities required under the law. Such decision be taken within one month from today. The application is thus, disposed of.
