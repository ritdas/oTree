ORAL
ORDER

(Per : HONOURABLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE MR. K.S.RADHAKRISHNAN) Heard learned counsel for the parties.

We have also perused the Report of the Expert Committee, constituted by this Court vide orders dated 22nd September and 6th October 2005 and in Special Civil Application No.2922 of 1995.

In paragraph No.6.3 of the Report, the Committee has opined as under:

?S6.3 The Committee reviewed the relevant information made available by the Board and the concerned industrial units to ascertain whether there has been degradation of Environment and damage caused to the environment by each of the units by not observing norms of the Board.

Central Pulp Mills Ltd., Surat.

(Presently :

J.K.Paper Ltd.) It was discharging large quantity of the trade effluent into the Nala which met the River Tapi. Though the industry has claimed for adequate treatment but in fact it had been exceeded several time higher than the permissible limit of the Norms during 1993-97. This violation of the norms cannot be condoned. Further more, that the water of River Tapi has been used as only source of drinking water for Surat City, located downstream of discharge point thus causing possible difficulties to water supply source of Surat. This mode of disposal is not desirable, from the environmental point of view.

Deepak Nitrite Ltd., Vadodara It was discharging large quantity of effluent into the River Mini and was not observing the Norms, because of inadequate pollution treatment facilities provided at that time, polluting the River Mini. This mode of disposal into River Mini is not acceptable. The degradation caused to the environment in the past cannot be condoned.

Baroda Dairy, Vadodara It is a milk processing unit and was discharging huge quantity of effluent without meeting the norms. This unit is surrounded by densely populated area and it had not provided adequate pollution control system at that time. The Board had also initiated legal actions for the violation of the provisions of the Water Act, 1974. The unit has now provided adequate pollution control systems, but earlier violation cannot be condoned.

Gujarat Torrent Bio-tech Ltd., Vadodara It is a pharmaceutical bulk drugs manufacturing industry and was discharging large quantity of effluent into the Effluent Channel, Vadodara, where relaxed norms are applicable. The industry was not meeting even the relaxed norms in view of inadequate treatment facilities provided and operated at that time. It has now updated effluent treatment facilities and shown improvement in meeting the norms. However, the unit has caused environmental pollution during the years under reference.

Minal Oil & Agro Industries, Mehsana (Presently: Kanak Castor Products Pvt. Ltd.) The industry was established without the consent of the Board and was discharging effluent on the land which caused stagnation and percolation and deteriorated the quality of ground water. It had violated norms of the Board simply because no pollution control facilities were provided at that time. This unit has ceased production later on. Now, new management has taken over this industrial unit.

Sushma Textiles, Bharuch This is a small scale textile processing industry and was discharging about 8000 litres/day of effluent without providing any treatment to its trade effluent. Initially it had carried out bleaching and dyeing process and generated effluent with high pH value and high colour units without obtaining required consent of the Board. The Board has also initiated legal action at that time. It has now changed to low pollution process but earlier violation cannot be condoned.

6.4 Compensation for Damage All the six units, have caused significant environmental pollution ? degradation, and therefore these units must compensate for such damage on the accepted principle ?SPolluter Pays??. In the absence of the feasibility of quantification of the damage to the environment by each unit, during the period under reference separately, the Committee considers that the damage and degradation to the environment by each industrial unit can be based on the quantity of the industrial waste water discharged by the unit and characteristics of the effluent violating the norms prescribed by the GPCB and other factors discussed earlier. In the absence of any other method, the degradation of the environment by each unit can quite reasonably be linked to the production capacity ? turnover of the unit during the period under reference and the percentage of the said turnover which could be levied from the industrial unit towards fine for damaging the environment.

The Committee therefore recommends that all the units under reference should pay the compensation based on the yearly production capacity ? turnover during the period under reference causing environmental pollution ? degradation.??

Learned counsel appearing for the petitioners has submitted that the Expert Committee Report could not specifically indicate as to what extent actual damage to the environment is being caused by the Industries in question, and therefore, it may not be appropriate for this Court to award compensation at the rate of 1% of the turnover, as it is not an appropriate formula. He, therefore, submits that the Report cannot be accepted as it is.

Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, we feel it appropriate to direct the Gujarat Pollution Control Board (GPCB) to examine the issue as to whether the industries in question are complying with the required standards set out by the GPCB, and whether they are causing any pollution and resultant damage to the environment. The GPCB shall conduct on the spot inspection of all the units in question and submit a report within a month from today.

Post this matter on 20th October, 2008.
