Heard the parties.

Apprehending his arrest, the petitioner has moved this Court for grant of privilege of anticipatory bail in connection with Chhattarpur P.S. Case No. 187 of 2018 registered under Sections 175/379/420 of the Indian Penal Code, section 25/26 (2) of Water Act, section 21 of the AIR Act, section 6 (i) of Factories Act, section 33 (B) and section 33 (c ) of Forest Act, section 4/54 (1) of JMMC Rule, 2004 and section 4/21 of MMDR Act, 1957. .

The learned counsel appearing for the petitioners submits that the allegations against the petitioners is that the petitioners have illegally stored stones chips, boulders which were seized and the petitioners could not produce any valid papers regarding crusher, chips, and boulders. It is then submitted that the allegations against the petitioners are all false and the petitioners have no concern with the seized articles. It is further submitted by learned counsel for the petitioners that the petitioner has been implicated in this case as he refused to put signature on a blank paper in some case instituted by the officers of the Government. It is next submitted that the petitioner is ready to co-operate with the investigation of the case and also ready and willing to furnish sufficient security including cash security and he undertakes not to be involved with any crusher machine nor will run any crusher machine during pendency of the case, hence, the petitioners be given the privilege of anticipatory bail.

The learned Addl. PP opposes the prayer for anticipatory bail of the petitioner.

Considering aforesaid facts and circumstances of the case, I am inclined to grant the privilege of anticipatory bail to the petitioner. Hence, in the event of arrest by the police or surrender within a period of four weeks from the date of this order, the petitioner shall be released on bail on depositing Rs. 20,000/- as cash security and furnishing bail bond of Rs. 25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned CJM, Palamau in connection with Chhattarpur P.S. Case No. 187 of 2018 subject to the condition that the petitioner will not get involved with any crusher machine nor will run any crusher during pendency of the case co-operate with the Investigation of the case and will appear before the Investigating Officer as and when noticed by him and will submit mobile number and photocopy of Aadhar card at the time of surrender in the court below with an undertaking not to change mobile number during the pendency of the case along with the other conditions laid down under section 438 (2) Cr. P.C.
