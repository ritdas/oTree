Arguments heard on the point of sentence.

It is submitted on behalf of convict that convict is aged about 65 years and he has a family to support. He had closed business activity in the said unit in 2000 itself as the cost of regular checkup of the plant was expensive and his business was not a big business thought he had purchased the ETP and got it installed. It was a small work and presently the unit is lying closed and an affidavit to that effect has been filed on record today. Convict is facing trial in the present matter for last about 15 years and in view of the ratio laid down in "A­one Automobile Case", a lenient view may kindly be taken.

On the other hand, it is submitted on behalf of the complainant that convict has been causing pollution by running/operating his unit of electroplating by discharging the direct untreated effluent to the public sewer/drain. Therefore, it DPCC vs M/s N.R. Industries U/s 24/25/26 r/w sec. 43/44 of Water Act CC No.199/06 1 is prayed that maximum punishment may be awarded to the convict as prescribed in the Act.

I have heard the rival contentions of both the parties. Now a days, Environment/Pollution has become cause for global concern. Every country across the world is working hard on ways to curb the ever escalating pollution in environment which is threatening the existence of human being, animals and trees on the Earth. Parliament, in bringing a new legislation for sound and betterment of Air Pollution, Water Pollution and Noise Pollution been hotly debating over the ways to provide clean and healthy environment to the citizens which are their fundamental rights. New laws are being enacted and new notification is issued regularly to effectively check the pollution levels in the atmosphere.

Delhi, the Capital of our country, is regarded as one of the most polluted cities in the world. If, the pollution in Delhi's atmosphere is not controlled, it would not be possible to live with a healthy life in Delhi. The people would start migrating to other parts of the country which are comparatively less polluted and Delhi would become a ghost city.

The Hon'ble Supreme Court explaining the gravity of the issue in the case of "UP Pollution Control Board vs. Dr. Bhupender Kumar Modi & Anr. (2009) 2 SCC 147", reiterated what was held by it about a decade ago in the case of "UP Pollution Control Board Vs. M/s Mohan Makin Ltd. & Ors. AIR 2000 SC 1456", observed as under:­ "Courts can not afford to lightly deal with cases involving pollution of air and water. The message must go to all concern. The courts will share the DPCC vs M/s N.R. Industries U/s 24/25/26 r/w sec. 43/44 of Water Act CC No.199/06 2 parliamentary concern on the escalating pollution level of our environment. Those who discharge noxious polluting effluents to streams may be unconcerned about the enormity of the injury which it inflicts on the public health at large, the irreparable impairment it causes on the aquatic organisms, the deleteriousness it imposes on the life and health of animals. So the Courts should not deal with the prosecution for offences under the Act in a casual or routine manner. Parliamentary concern in the matter is adequately reflected in strengthening the measures prescribed by the statute. The court has no jurisdiction for ignoring the seriousness of the subject".

Having heard the rival submissions of both the parties, considering the age of the convict, period of trial and ratio laid down in the case of "DPCC vs A­One Automobile" on the point of sentence, considering the gravity of the offence and tomorrow being the 'Earth Day', this court is of the considered opinion that following sentences will meet the ends of justice.

(a) Convict is sentenced to pay damages of Rs.10,000/­ to the complainant; (b) Convict is directed to spend Rs.40,000/­ towards displaying cloth banners, hoardings and pamphlet for public awareness mentioning therein the following "slogans" written by the AR of the complainant, out side his business premises/residential premises/at conspicuous places.
