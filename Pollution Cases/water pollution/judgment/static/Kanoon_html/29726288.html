                             ORAL ORDER

(PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE AKIL KURESHI) Petitioner has challenged the order dated 26.3.2013 as at Annexure A to the petition under which the petitioner was directed to stop the industrial activity at its unit located at Survey No.76 to 81 of village Dari, Taluka Patan, Dist: Junagadh. In the said order it was conveyed that electricity connection would be discontinued and even if the petitioner was running the industry with its captive power plant, the same should also be discontinued. Such order was passed after giving notice and hearing to the petitioner.

From the affidavit in reply filed by the GPCB, it emerges that the petitioner was not complying with the environmental norms.

Page 1 of 3

C/SCA/7222/2013 ORDER The petitioner did not have consolidated consent and authorization which was valid upto 10th March 2009. Since the petitioner was not complying with the environmental norms, such authorization was not continued thereafter. GPCB has also stated that currently the petitioner is carrying on such activity in violation of the provisions of section 25 of the Water Act, section 21 of the Air Act and relevant provisions of Hazardous Waste Rules framed under the Environment Protection Act, 1986. It is also stated that the activity is being carried out within 400 meters of coastal area and hence was also required to obtain clearance under CRZ Rules.

Though affidavit in rejoinder is filed to such reply filed by the GPCB, these vital aspects are not seriously disputed. The petitioner's main contention appears to be that the GPCB did not grant clearance without proper reasons and only to stop the petitioner's production activity.

Under the circumstances, we have no reason to interfere with the impugned order passed by the GPCB. The petitioner carrying on industrial activity was required to obtain necessary authorization from GPCB. As an industrial unit within 400 meters of the coastal region, it also required CRZ clearances. In absence of such statutory requirements, the petitioner cannot continue its industrial activity. Merely by stating that GPCB only with a view to stopping the activity did not grant such clearance would not be sufficient.

In view of the above discussion, this petition is dismissed.
