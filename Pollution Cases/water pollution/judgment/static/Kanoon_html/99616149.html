                                       ORDER

(Order delivered by the Bench) This application is brought-forth by the appellant in Appeal No. 7 of 2012 (SZ) seeking condonation of delay of 84 days in preferring this appeal No. 7 of 2012 (SZ) whereby the applicant has sought for the quashing the order of the 1st respondent made on 30th July 2012 confirming an order of the 3rd respondent passed in Appeal No. 95 of 2012 dated 16.07.2012. The Tribunal heard the learned counsel for the applicant and also for the respondent Nos. 2 and 3.

2) Since this is an application seeking condonation of delay, it is not necessary to place the facts of the case except stating that the applicant was running a stone crushing unit and the 3rd respondent made an order dated 16.7.2010 for the closure of the unit. The applicant challenged the same before the Appellate Authority, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control under the provisions of the Water Act and the said appeal was rejected on 30.7.2012. Aggrieved over the same, the applicant filed a writ petition before the High Court of Madras and the same was returned on different dates and finally on 4.10.2012 and under the said circumstances, he has preferred this appeal wherein a delay of 84 days had occasioned. Seeking condonation of the delay, this application is filed. The only question which would arise for consideration is whether this delay of 84 days needs to be condoned. Admittedly, the order of the Appellate Authority under challenge came to be passed on 30.7.2012. The said order was received by the applicant on 10.08.2012 and hence the appeal should have been preferred within 30 days from the date of receipt of the order, i.e., on or before 09.09.2012. In the instant case, the appeal has been filed only on 17.11.2012 and there was a delay of 84 days in preferring the instant appeal.

Page 2 of 4

3) It is contended by the learned counsel for the applicant that after passing of the order by the Appellate Authority, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control on 30.07.2012 and received by the applicant on 10.8.2012, the applicant filed a writ petition before the High Court of Madras on 07.09.2012 and the said writ petition was returned on 10.8.2012 for the first time and finally on 4.10.2012 on the last representation. Thus the applicant has exercised his diligence and care in preferring the writ petition for quashing the order under challenge and thus he can avail the benefit of Section 14 of the Limitation Act since he has initiated proceedings before another forum on the bonafide belief. The learned counsel has relied on the Apex Court judgment in (2008) 7 SCC page No. 169, (Consolidated Engineering Enterprises Vs. Principal Secretary, Irrigation Department and others) and he appeals that the delay has got to be condoned.

4) The learned counsel for the respondent No. 2 would submit that the application has to be dismissed since the delay is not only on the higher side but also inordinate.

5) After hearing the contentions putforth by either side, the Tribunal is of the considered opinion that the application has to be allowed. As could to be seen from the available materials, the copy of the order made by the first respondent dated 30.7.2012 was received by the applicant only on 10.8.2012. The applicant should have preferred the appeal within 30 days, the period available to prefer an appeal under the provisions of NGT Act. But he has not done so. In the instant case, it is also noticed that the appeal was not preferred even within 60 days period in which case the delay could be condoned by the Tribunal, if sufficient causes are brought to the notice of the Tribunal. But the appeal is preferred only on 17.11.2012. Thus there was a delay of 84 days beyond the said period of 90 days.

6) The Tribunal is able to notice the following circumstance which stands in favour of the applicant. After the receipt of the order under challenge on 10.8.2012, the applicant has preferred a writ petition to quash the order before the High Court of Madras on 7.9.2012. As seen from the order of return, the last return of writ petition was made on 4.10.2012 by the High Court of Madras. It is quite evident that the Page 3 of 4 applicant instead of preferring an appeal before the Tribunal, has chosen another forum and filed writ petition and thus it is a case where the applicant can have the benefit of Section 14 of the Limitation Act. Apart from the same, the judgment of the Apex Court made in (2008) 7 SCC page No. 169, (Consolidated Engineering Enterprises Vs. Principal Secretary, Irrigation Department and others) and relied on by the applicant stands to the benefit of the applicant. From the reading of the judgment, it can be seen that the said period which was taken by the party in preferring the proceedings in another forum but with bona fide belief is not extended by the court, but it has only excluded the period of limitation and thus in view of the above and that too in a matter connected to and concerned with environment, the opportunity of being heard is given to the applicant and the said delay has got to be condoned with costs. A cost of Rs.2,500/- is to be remitted by the applicant to the Bar Association of National Green Tribunal, Southern Zone, Chennai on or before 04.03.2013. The matter is posted to 06.03.2013.
