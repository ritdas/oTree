Both these writ petitions are being taken up for hearing together as similar issues are involved in these two proceedings. The writ petitioner in W.P. No. 2994(W) of 2013 has applied for electricity connection in Nabagram block in the district of Murshidabad. The process for effecting supply appears to have been held up for non- furnishing of certificate under the provisions of West Bengal Ground Water Resources (Management, Control and Regulation) Act, 2005 (the Water Act, in short). An affidavit has been filed by the Director, State Water Investigation Directorate and Chairman, State Level Ground Water Development Authority. In this affidavit the manner in which license under the aforesaid Act is granted has been explained.

It is submitted by the learned Senior Standing Counsel that the present policy of the State is to require certificates from all applicants who want to use subsoil water through a submersible pump energized by electricity. In respect of blocks in this State categorised as critical and semi-critical, rigorous test is applied, but for regular blocks, a liberalised procedure is pursued.

Learned Counsel appearing for the petitioners in both the writ 2 petitions submits that in both the cases, applications have been made before the Water Authority. Let these applications be processed, and actual connection shall be withheld till registration certificates from the said authority are obtained. This Court would request the Water authorities to take decision on the petitioners' applications within a period of six weeks from the date of communication of this order.

On such certificate being produced, within a period of six weeks from the dates of production of certificate, supply shall be effected to the petitioners. In the event for any compelling reason such supply cannot be effected within the timeframe prescribed above, then the petitioners or any one of them, as the case may be, shall be informed in writing the reason for delay.

So far as W.P. No. 2994(W) of 2013 is concerned, it is submitted on behalf of the distribution company that connection was given to the petitioner from a common transformer on the basis of his undertaking that he shall produce the certificate from the Water Authority. In the event the Water Authority grants certificate, then supply shall be regularised. If such certificate is refused, then there shall be disconnection of supply.

The writ petitions stand disposed of in the above terms. There shall, however be, no order as to costs. Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, shall be supplied to the learned Counsel for the parties as expeditiously as possible, in compliance of usual formalities. 3 (Aniruddha Bose, J.)
