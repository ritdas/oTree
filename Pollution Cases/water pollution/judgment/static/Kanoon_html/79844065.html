Hon'ble Anant Kumar,J.

Vakalatnama filed on behalf of opposite party no.2 by Shri Kuldeep Srivastava, Advocate is taken on record.

It is stated by learned counsel for the petitioner that petitioner has been summoned vide summoning order dated 28.02.2012, in Complaint Case No.369 of 2011 (U.P. Pollution Control Board Vs. M/s Shopper's Pride mall cum Multiplex & others) to face trial under Sections 41, 43 & 44 of the Water Pollution Act. It is stated by learned counsel for the petitioner that petitioner is noway connected with the Shopper's Pride mall cum Multiplex. In fact he is a Company Secretary and as per the requirement of provisions of law, petitioner had appended his signature on the incorporation of the company before the registration of company. So, he is in no way connected with the company. Learned counsel for the opposite party no.2 has stated that this controversy cannot be settled at this stage as the same can be looked into by the trial court itself.

At this stage, learned counsel for the petitioner prays for and is permitted to move an application for discharge before the trial court concerned through counsel within a period of one month.

Accordingly, the petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. is disposed of with a direction to the trial court concerned that in case petitioner moves an application for discharge through counsel within a period of one month, the same shall be decided expeditiously.

Till disposal of the application for discharge, no coercive action shall be taken against the applicant/petitioner in pursuance of the proceedings in Complaint Case No.369 of 2011 (U.P. Pollution Control Board Vs. M/s Shopper's Pride mall cum Multiplex & others).

Disposed of.
