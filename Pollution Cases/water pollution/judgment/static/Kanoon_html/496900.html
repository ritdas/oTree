ORAL ORDER

(Per : HONOURABLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE MR. S.J. MUKHOPADHAYA) The writ petition in public interest has been filed by the petitioner for a direction to the respondent authorities to stop the activities of the 12th respondent of establishing the industrial unit at village Nani Tambadi, District Valsad. Apart from the above plea, it is also pleaded that the small agriculturists in the area have spent huge amount for development of their land. Now due to demolition of the sub-canals while levelling the land by the 12th respondent, the irrigation facilities to their fields will be disturbed. It is also alleged that no prior permission has been taken from the competent authority.

2. This Court while issued notice on the respondents on 2.7.2010, prohibited the 12th respondent from making any further construction on the land purchased by it. The said interim order was allowed to continue subject to the order that could have been passed in the petition for vacating stay preferred by the 12th respondent.

3. The learned counsel appearing on behalf of the 12th respondent pressed the petition for vacating the interim order of stay.

4. We have heard the parties in detail.

5. It appears that the Gujarat Pollution Control Board has granted conditional permission under the Water Act, 1974 and the Air Act, 1981 wherein the following conditions have been laid down:-

1. This order is subject to the outcome of the final order of Honourable High Court in SCA No. 6233 of 2010.

2. You can establish industrial plant only to manufacture TEXTURIZED YARN.

3. You must obtain all necessary approvals from various government departments before to start construction.

4. You must obtain N.A. permission from competent before to start construction activities.

5. You will have to obtain the environmental clearance from the competent authority for the products which required prior E.C. before to start construction activities for that product.

6. You have to take all necessary precautions to control noise pollution. You will not carry out any activity which may crease pollution related problem in surrounding area.

6. Per contra, the learned senior counsel appearing on behalf of the 12th respondent submits that the N.A. permission as has been ordered to obtain is not required to be obtained in the present case. Further, according to him, the environmental clearance from the competent authority is also not required as the products which the 12th respondent intends to make do not require prior environmental clearance before starting up the production activities.

7. The Collector, Valsad in his affidavit has given details of land being Survey Nos. 102, 103, 104/1, 104/2, 105, 106 and 121 of village Nani Tambadi, Taluka Pardi, District Valsad. It is stated that the 12th respondent intimated that they have started bona fide industrial use as per Section 63AA of the Gujarat Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 and they are required to intimate within 30 days as per the Government of Gujarat Ordinance No. 3 of 1996 dated 26.6.1996. Now the matter has been intimated to the Collector by letter dated 25.2.2010. As per Section 65B of the Bombay Land Revenue Code and Government Ordinance No. 2 of 1996 dated 26.6.1996, the 12th respondent occupant required to use the land for bona fide industrial use is not required to take prior permission from the Collector. However, the 12th respondent is under an obligation to intimate within 30 days and the post facto N.A permission is required to be obtained after scrutinizing the necessary papers. After the intimation was given by the 12th respondent on 25.4.2010, a large number of objections appears to have been received from the villagers. Therefore, no post facto N.A. Permission is granted till date.

8. Taking into consideration the aforesaid facts, while we are not inclined to vacate the interim order of stay for the present, allow the 12th respondent to move before the competent authority to obtain necessary permission as per the conditions imposed by the Gujarat Pollution Control Board. If such permission is sought for from the competent authority, including the permission as already sought for from the Collector, Valsad, the authorities will consider and decide the same in accordance with law during pendency of the case and any such decision shall be subject to the decision in this case. It is expected that the Collector, Valsad will decide the matter on the application of the 12th respondent taking into consideration the objections raised by the villagers on an early date, preferably within one month.

This Civil Application stands disposed of.
