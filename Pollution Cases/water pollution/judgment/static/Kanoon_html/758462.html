Background

1. The Appellant filed his RTI application dt.29.5.08 with the CPIO, M/o Envt. & Forests, Bhopal. He requested for following information with regard to some of Gujarat based industries, a list of which he enclosed along with RTI application.

i) Environmental Clearance issued by Ministry of Environment and Forest (MoEF), New Delhi to these industries.

ii) Compliance report of conditions of Environmental Clearance submitted by these industries

iii) Visit report of visit carried out by regional office of MoEF to each industry.

iv) Analysis reports of air/water/solid waste samples carried out by regional office of MoEF in each industry.

v) Environmental Management Plan submitted by each industry.

vi) Show cause notice/closure notice ever issued by MoEF or GPCB under Water Act 1974/Air Act 1981/Environmental Protection Act 1986 and other rules thereof to these industries.

The CPIO, MoEF, Bhopal vide letter dt.17.6.08 requested the Appellant that the DD be addressed to MoEF, New Delhi. Accordingly, the Appellant sent the DD to MoEF, New Delhi vide letter dt.21.6.08. The CPIO replied on 10.7.08 as follows:

i) W.r.t list of industries provided in the Annexure along with the RTI application, it is to mention that S.Nos.4,10 and 14 have been granted a number of Environmental Clearances to various projects. Since information sought in respect of these three industries constitute approximately 1500- 2000 pages found in a number of files and would disproportionately divert the resources of the public authority, he requested the applicant to furnish specific project for which information is to be provided.

ii) He directed the applicant to pay Rs.220/- for providing copies of EC & EIA/EMP with regard to S.No.17 which is available as per office records.

iii) Remaining industries as mentioned in the Annexure do not have ECs as per office records Not satisfied with the reply, the Appellant filed an appeal dt.14.8.08 with the Appellate Authority. The Appellate Authority replied on 3.9.08 stating that since the Appellant vide his letter dt.29.8.08 has expressed to skip the demand of EMP out of his six requirements made originally, CPIO may provide the information as per the modified requirement of the applicant. The Appellant then filed a second appeal dt.2.12.08 before the CIC.

2. The Bench of Mrs. Annapurna Dixit, Information Commissioner, scheduled the hearing for February 23, 2009.

3. Mr. A.K. Rana, IFS, Regional CCF, Bhopal & Dr. A. Mehrotra, Director, & R.O.

MOEF, Bhopal represented the Public Authority.

4. The Applicant was not present during the hearing.

Decision

5. The Respondents submitted that most of the Industries mentioned in the list provided by the Appellant do not have the Environment Clearance since they had started functioning before the Act came into being. With regard to some of the Industries implementing a number of projects, the Respondents stated that they needed to get more clarification from the Appellant regarding the project the Appellant is referring to, to enable them to provide the information.

6. The Commission noted that information against Points 1 (a) of the RTI request has already been provided. The Appellant is advised to be more specific about the projects against which he is seeking Environmental clearance against Industries No.4, 7, 10,11,12 and 14 in the list provided in order to reduce the volume of information which otherwise would have to be compiled. Compliance reports against Point 1(b) to be provided; however, only based on the specific projects identified by the Appellant against Industries provided by the Appellant for the industries 4, 7,10,11,12 & 14. As regards point (c) information is denied under section 8(i)(d) since according to the field reports contain information which is of commercial confidence including production details, quality of raw material, water consumption, disposal of affluent quantity etc. With regard to point (d), the Respondent submitted that analysis reports are prepared by the Industries and submitted by them to the Ministry and such analysis is not carried out by any Regional office of the Ministry. With regard to Point (e) the CPIO is advised to allow the Appellant to inspect the concerned documents and to provide him with certified copies of the information required. All information to be provided within 20 days of the receipt of this Order.

7. The appeal is disposed off.
