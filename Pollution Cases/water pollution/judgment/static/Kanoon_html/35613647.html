                              ORDER

In the normal course, this Court would have relegated the petitioner to the remedy of appeal under clause (c) of Section 33B of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 (for short 'the said Act of 1974'). However, the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner, on instructions, states that the petitioner is confining the prayer in the petition only to one direction regarding disposal of the representation dated 23rd September 2019 (Annexure-L). His contention is that in similar matters, the direction issued under Section 33A of the said Act of 1974 has been modified by the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board.

2. In view of the limited prayer, we dispose of the petition by passing the following order:

(i) The second respondent is directed to decide the representation dated 23rd September 2019 (Annexure-L) in accordance with law;
(ii) The representation shall be decided and the orders passed thereon shall be communicated to the petitioner as expeditiously as possible 4 and in any event, within a period of four weeks from today;
(iii) All contentions on merits are kept open.
