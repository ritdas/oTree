                             ORDER

1.This application is filed under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

2.The petitioners herein are the accused Nos. 1 to 3 in Crime No.458 of 2017 of the Nilambur Police Station, registered alleging offences punishable under Sections 270 of the IPC, section 120(e) of the Kerala Police Act, Sections 24 and 43 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and under Section 3(2)(a) of the Prevention of Damages to Public Property Act, 1984.

3.According to the prosecution, sometime between 22 hours on 13.10.2017 and 6 hours on 14.10.2017, the petitioners herein, who are the owner and employees of two lorries, collected waste materials and other human excreta from various places and dumped it in the river near Vadappuram bridge and thus contaminated the water body. The petitioners were arrested BA 8528/2017 2 on 7.11.2017 and has been in custody since then.

4.Heard the learned counsel for the petitioners, who asserts that the petitioners are innocent.

5.This submission is countered by the learned Public Prosecutor who submits that the acts committed by the petitioners herein were recorded in the Close Circuit TV and the materials sourced out during investigation clearly shows the complicity of the petitioners.

6.I have considered the submissions advanced and have gone through the materials on record. Though the allegations against the petitioners are extremely grave, having regard to the fact that they have been undergoing detention since 7.11.2017, I am of the view that they can now be enlarged on bail.

7.In the result this application will stand allowed. However, it shall be subjected to the following conditions.

1). The petitioners shall be released on bail on their executing a bond for Rs.50,000/-(Rupees Fifty thousand only) each with two solvent sureties each for the like sum to the satisfaction of the court having jurisdiction.

BA 8528/2017 3

2). The petitioners shall appear before the Investigating Officer on all Saturdays between 9 a.m. and 1 p.m., for 2 months or till final report is filed, whichever is earlier.

3). The petitioners shall not intimidate or attempt to influence the witnesses; nor shall they tamper with the evidence.

4). The petitioners shall not commit any offence while they are on bail.

In case of violation of any of the above conditions, the jurisdictional Court shall be empowered to consider the application for cancellation, if any, and pass appropriate orders in accordance with the law.
