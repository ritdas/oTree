                       J U D G M E N T

The petitioners were aggrieved with the order passed by the Kerala State Pollution Control Board (PCB), refusing consent to establish, produced as Ext.P9. Ideally, the petitioners ought to have challenged the same before the Appellate Authority constituted under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act 1981 (for brevity 'the Air Act). The petitioners also filed the same as is indicated from Ext.P12, produced subsequently along with the interlocutory application. However, the consideration was not be made, since there was no appellate authority constituted as per the Air Act. As of now, the Government has constituted the appellate authority by notification G.O(P) No.5/2016/Envt. 2 W.P.(C) No.31063 of 2016- G dated 20.12.2016. In such circumstance, there shall be a direction that the matter shall be taken up and shall be considered for any interim orders, within a period of one month from the date of receipt of a certified copy of this judgment.

Leaving open all contentions, the writ petition would stand disposed of.
