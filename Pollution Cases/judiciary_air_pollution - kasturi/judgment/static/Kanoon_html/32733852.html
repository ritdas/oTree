     THIS WRIT PETITION IS FILED UNDER ARTICLES 226 AND

DIRECTION IN WP NO.60404/2009 DATED 02.08.2010 AND ETC.,

     THIS WRIT PETITION COMING ON FOR PRELIMINARY
HEARING THIS DAY, CHIEF JUSTICE MADE THE FOLLOWING:

                           ORDER

D.H.WAGHELA, C.J. (Oral) :

1. Pursuant to the order dated 24.03.2014, learned counsel for respondent No.2 has filed with a memo, the documents related to analysis reports of the ambient air quality in and around the area in question for the purpose of determining whether respondent No.3 is consistently releasing pollutants beyond the permissible limit and whether respondent No.2 is required to take any preventive and regulatory measures.

2. Upon a copy of the memo being supplied to learned counsel for the petitioner, he submitted that according to the reports about ambient air quality, the norms prescribed by the Pollution Control Board under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, do not appear to have been crossed by respondent No.3 during the period of observation and taking -3- of samples. He, therefore, submitted that in case there is persistent pollution in the subsequent period and any damage on that account and if the petitioner still insists that air pollution continues on account of working of the industry of respondent No.3, the petitioner will collect necessary material and data having evidentiary value for the purpose of making a complaint before the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board. He further submitted that in case of such complaint not being acted upon by the State Pollution Control Board in a legal and effective manner, the right of the petitioner or such other affected persons may be reserved to approach this Court.

3. The petition is disposed accordingly as withdrawn, with liberty as aforesaid, with no order as to cost.
