                            ORDER

Petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C.

2. The petitioner is the respondent in M.C. No.1 of 2018 on the file of the Sub Divisional Magistrate, Thrissur which was initiated under Section 133 Cr.PC. The Sub Divisional Magistrate issued Annexure-A1 conditional order. The proceedings were initiated on the basis of Annexure-A2 complaint filed by the 3rd respondent. Annexures-A1 and A2 are sought to be quashed on the ground that with regard to the very same issue there are provisions in the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and hence no proceedings can be initiated under Section 133 Cr.P.C. Crl.M.C. 2845/18 -:2:-

3. Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner the third respondent and the learned Public Prosecutor.

4. The learned counsel for the petitioner relies on the decision of the learned single Judge of this Court in Tata Tea Ltd. v. State of Kerala (1984 KLT 645) in support of his argument that Section 133 Cr.P.C. has been impliedly repealed by the provisions in the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. This decision has been overruled in Krishna Panicker v. Appukuttan Nair (1993 (1) KLT 771. There is no merit in the argument advanced by the learned counsel for the petitioner.

In the result, this Crl.M.C. is dismissed.
