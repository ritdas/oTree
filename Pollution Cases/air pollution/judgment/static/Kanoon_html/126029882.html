                                JUDGMENT

The petitioner has, in the instant writ petition, sought for many reliefs but, during the course of argument, confined her prayer for issuance of an appropriate direction to the Appellate Authority, established under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, to take a decision on the appeal preferred against the order of the Kerala Pollution Control Board withdrawing the permission/consent granted on the premise that the petitioner was permitted to keep 250 chicken but exceeded the same. As a consequence thereon, the Secretary of Chathamangalam Panchayath issued the impugned Ext.P13 stop memo dated 26.09.2019.

2. Having heard the learned counsel for the petitioner, I am of the view that this writ petition can be disposed of with a direction to the 3 rd respondent Appellate Authority to decide the appeal preferred against the order of the competent authority, withdrawing the WP(C).No.28532 OF 2019(N) 3 permission/consent, as the decision would have a consequential effect upon the sustainability of the impugned order herein.

Let the Appellate Authority decide the appeal within a period of two months from the date of receipt of a certified copy of the judgment. In case the petitioner succeeds in the appeal the Secretary of the Chathamangalam Grama Panchayath will re-consider the issue, after affording an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner.

The writ petition stands disposed of.
