COMMON ORDER

(Order of the Court was made by THE HON'BLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE)

		Learned Counsel for the petitioner submits that he does not seek to assail the impugned order of closure, but the limited relief he seeks in the present petitions is that the seals be opened so as to facilitate the removal of the machinery and relocation somewhere else with compliance.

		2.In view of the aforesaid limited prayer, learned Counsel appearing for the respondents 2 and 3 submits that qua the aforesaid aspect, a direction would have to be issued to the District Collector of Kancheepuram District as the sealing is carried out by the said authority.

		3.We thus direct the District Collector of Kancheepuram District to de-seal the premises on a written request being made by the petitioner for the purpose of removal of machinery whereafter the petitioner may use it for a purpose for which the premises is permissible to be used as the premises is stated to be owned by the petitioner.

		4.Since the learned Counsel for the petitioner states that two months time would be required for removal of the machinery, the premises would be inspected by the District Collector after de-sealing on lapse of two months time to ensure that the machinery has been removed.

		5.The writ petitions accordingly stand disposed of.  No costs.  Consequently, connected MPs stand closed.
